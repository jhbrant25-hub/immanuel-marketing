The Formation Series

Not a devotional.
A field manual.

The Immanuel Men Field Manual Series is a formation curriculum built on the conviction that Christian men deserve more than feel-good theology. Each volume draws from ancient patristic sources, Hebrew and Greek word studies, Second Temple literature, and the lived reality of men who walk with God in ordinary life — and extraordinary fire.


The enemy has weapons. Worry fractures the mind. Fear paralyzes the man. Control corrupts the leader. Each manual names the weapon, traces it to its root, and equips the man to move from passive survivor to Warrior-Priest.


This is ancient theology. This is the field.

The Author

John Brantley

John Brantley is a theologian, formation writer, and founder of Immanuel Men Ministry. His work draws from the patristic tradition of the ancient Church — the Greek Orthodox East, the Philokalia tradition of the Russian Orthodox, and the 81-book canon of the Ethiopian Orthodox Tewahedo Church — alongside rigorous Hebrew and Greek scholarship and Second Temple literature.


His writing is built on a single theological north star: We choose to love Jesus — as He loves us. Every volume in the Immanuel Men series is a deployment of that conviction into the specific battles men face: worry, fear, control, identity, calling, and the light they were commissioned to carry.
